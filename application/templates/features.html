Features

PoseCheck uses 6 primary features to monitor and assess users' pose, tracking baseline and abnormal values in order to provide correctional alerts and improve working health. These features are also combined to produce an overall posture summary feature.

Proximity

Measures how close your face is to the screen. Sitting in a way such that your laptop screen is very close to your face can cause long term strain and damage your eye health.

Measures distance from shoulders to ears. If you are slumping downwards, your shoulders will be closer to your ears. Slouching can cause your spinal discs to compress, and increases the likelihood of back pain.

Slump

Forward Tilt

Measures distance between your ears and your eyes. If the ears move to become higher than the pupils, the user is leaning their head further forwards. This can place undue strain on the back of your neck.

Measures difference in heights of your eyes. Leaning towards one side can place extra pressure on your neck, and can cause back and shoulder pain over long periods of time.

Head Tilt

Shoulder Tilt

Measures difference in heights of your shoulders. Leaning towards one side, or placing extra pressure on one shoulder can cause back and shoulder pain over long periods of time.

Measures the distance between your left and right shoulders, scaled for proximity. Narrowing your shoulders can be a sign that you are slouching forwards, or pushing your shoulder blades back. Either of these can cause strain and back pain over long periods of time.

Shoulder Width

Overall Pose

The overall score combines your scaled scores from each of the six features into one comprehensive measurement of how good your posture is.
